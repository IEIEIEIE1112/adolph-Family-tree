Adolph Family Legacy

Joseph Patrick Adolph & Agnes Gunn

Joseph P. Adolph (1882–1958) and Agnes Gunn (1893–1971) married and led their family on the Colville Reservation near Nespelem, WA. Both were active Tribal Council members and community pillars.

Descendants

Children of Joseph & Agnes

  • Eva Adolph (1911–2006): Sinixt elder, repatriation advocate.
  • William “Shortie” Adolph (1914–1963): WWII PFC.
  • Anna “Annie” Adolph (1916–1916): Infant.
  • Marie Mary Adolph (1918–1978): Married Goujon/St. Paul.
  • Ambrose Adolph (1921–2012): D‑Day veteran.
  • Vincent “Beanie” Adolph Sr. (1923–1964): Tribal leader.
  • Rose Adolph Squetimkin (1926–1983): Community figure.

Grandchildren (Children of Vincent Sr.)

  • Arlene B. Adolph (1953–1997): Educator and tribal advocate.
  • Donlyn M. Adolph (1954–2005): Housing Authority director.
  • Inez Reya Adolph (b. c.1956): Brewster community leader.
  • Sunny C. Adolph (d. 2023): Omak resident.
  • Phyllis Adolph (1962–2022): Nurse and mentor.
  • Kathleen “Kate” Sanchez (b. c.1960): Tribal council candidate.
