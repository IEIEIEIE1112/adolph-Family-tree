Adolph Family Legacy

Joseph Patrick Adolph & Agnes Gunn

Joseph Patrick Adolph (1882–1958) and Agnes Gunn (1893–1971) married and established their family near Nespelem, Washington, on the Colville Indian Reservation. As members of the Colville Confederated Tribes (Agnes belonging to the Lakes/Sinixt band), their lives were deeply connected to the region's landscape and community. Joseph actively participated in tribal governance, serving on the tribal business council. Public records, such as the obituary for their great-granddaughter Naomi Price, confirm their lineage and role as progenitors of this family branch:contentReference[oaicite:27]{index=27}.

Community Ties: The family has strong ties to the communities across the Colville Reservation. Public records indicate several family members, such as John Adolph Sr. (1902-1983) and Mary E Augusta Adolph (1901-1983), are buried at St. Mary's Mission Catholic Cemetery in Omak. More recent obituaries also show connections to Keller, with services for Lee Ambrose Adolph (1957-2023) held at the Keller Community Center. Ryan William Adolph-Louie (1983-2022), also buried at St. Mary's Mission Cemetery, is another individual connected to the Omak community noted in public records.
